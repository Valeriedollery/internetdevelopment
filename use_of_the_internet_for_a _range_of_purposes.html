Use of the Internet for a range of purposes

The Internet is used for communication, research, collaboration and e-Commerce.

Voice communication is audio conferencing, voice conferencing and VoIP (voice over internet protocol). Audio is two computers connected to speakers and a microphone using an instant messaging programme such as AOL and conferencing software such as Skype.

Video conferencing is similar to audio but has a webcam added to allow the users to see each other. This option can allow, depending on the service used (Teams or Zoom), many people to be seen at the same time. VoIP is a phone service to communicate over the internet instead of a traditional landline Skype, blueface).

Written communication is through email, instant messaging, IRC and SMS & wireless communications.


E-mail (electronic mail) is the exchange of messages from one user to another/others over the internet, the recipients e-mail address is needed, software such as Microsoft Outlook, an Internet Service Provider to support gateways and you can send e-mails to users anywhere in the world.


Instant Messaging (IM) is a form of online chat that allows immediate messages over the internet. These messages can be between two or more individuals and is in real-time. This service allows employees of a company instant access to each other, in different work areas or offices and also to managers. Information can be received or given immediately.


IRC (Internet Relay Chat) is a text-based chat system for instant messaging. It allows group communication on channels or rooms. An IRC client such as a miRC and Internet access is required see to join a discussion. The IRC server makes sure all messages are received and broadcast to all participants in the discussion. Messages can also be sent privately to one participant.


SMS & Wireless Communication (Short message service) a text messaging service component of telephone, internet and mobile devices.  It allows messages between mobiles and e-mails and voice-mail systems. Messages can have 160 alpha-numeric characters, no graphics or pictures.


Multimedia Messaging Service (MMS) followed which allowed pictures, graphics, videos and sound to be sent to other devices.  

File Sharing allows data to be shared publicly or privately over the internet. Files, folders, images, music and videos can be shared with individuals or organisations. Multiple people can use the files and there are multiple levels of access available to use on these files. Dropbox and Google Drive are file sharing services.

File Downloading, peer-to-peer (P2P) file sharing is the use of software that connects to a peer-to-peer network searching for shared files on the computer of another user. Large files such as books, games and movies can be downloaded from other peers. Programmes such as Bit Torrent and Limewire are used for P2P file sharing.

File hosting services are a simpler alternative to P2P software. It’s an internet hosting service designed to host user files. It allows users to upload files to be accessed over the internet when a username and password is used.

File sync and sharing are cloud-based services to implement automated file transfer. These files can be accessed from anywhere on other devices and can be easily shared with other users. Google Drive and Dropbox are common services used.

E-Commerce is the buying and selling of goods and services over the internet. These transactions are business to business (B2B) buying and selling, business to consumer (B2C) sales, consumer to consumer (C2C) sales and consumer to business (C2B) reviews.
